Traffic Light Rules

Mastering the signals is key to road safety. Explore the guide below.

Standard Signal Rules

🔴 Red Light

Meaning: Complete Stop.

Action: Stop behind the white limit line or crosswalk. Do not proceed until the light turns green.

Note: You may turn right on red after stopping, unless a sign forbids it.

🟡 Yellow Light

Meaning: Caution / The light is about to turn Red.

Action: Stop if you can do so safely. If you are already in the intersection, continue through carefully. Do not speed up.

🟢 Green Light

Meaning: Go.

Action: Proceed, but you must yield to any pedestrians or vehicles already in the intersection.

⬅️ Green Arrow

Meaning: Protected Turn.

Action: You have the right of way to turn in the direction of the arrow. Oncoming traffic is stopped.

Special Situations

  • Flashing Red: Treat this exactly like a Stop Sign. Stop completely, look all ways, then proceed when safe.
  • Flashing Yellow: Proceed with caution. You do not need to stop, but be alert for cross traffic or pedestrians.
  • Dead Signal (Power Outage): If the lights are completely dark, treat the intersection as a 4-Way Stop.
