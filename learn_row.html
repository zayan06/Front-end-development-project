The Master Guide to Right of Way

Right of way isn't something you "take"—it's something you yield. Master these rules to predict traffic and avoid collisions.

1. The 4-Way Intersection

When you approach an intersection governed by STOP signs in all directions, order matters.

🥇 The Golden Rule: First to Arrive The vehicle that arrives at the limit line and comes to a complete stop first gets to proceed first.
🤝 The "Right" Rule: Simultaneous Arrival If two vehicles arrive at the exact same time, the vehicle on the right has the right of way.
⬆️ Head-to-Head Arrival If two vehicles arrive at the exact same time facing each other (head-on), the vehicle going straight goes first. A vehicle turning left must yield to a vehicle going straight.

2. T-Intersections & Driveways

Not all intersections look like a perfect cross. When a minor road meets a major road, different rules apply.

T-Intersections

At a T-intersection without signs or signals, the road that "ends" must yield to the road that continues (the through-street). Vehicles on the through-street have the continuous right of way.

Driveways & Parking Lots

If you are exiting a driveway, an alley, or a parking lot onto a public road, you have the lowest priority. You must yield to all pedestrians on the sidewalk and all traffic on the main road before pulling out.

3. Unprotected Left Turns

Making a left turn on a solid green light (without a green arrow) causes a large percentage of intersection accidents.

⚠️ Yield to Oncoming Traffic

When turning left on a solid green light, you must yield to oncoming traffic going straight or turning right. You may only initiate your turn when there is a safe gap in oncoming traffic.

4. Roundabouts (Traffic Circles)

Roundabouts are designed to keep traffic flowing without stopping, reducing severe T-bone accidents by 80%.

Entering the Circle

Traffic entering the roundabout must yield to traffic already inside the circle. Do not stop inside a roundabout; keep moving.

Choosing Your Lane

In a multi-lane roundabout, choose your lane before entering. Generally, use the right lane to turn right or go straight, and the left lane to turn left or make a U-turn.

5. The Unbreakable Exceptions

No matter what the traffic light or street sign says, you must immediately yield the right of way to these specific groups:

🚑

Emergency Vehicles

If an ambulance, police car, or fire truck is approaching with lights and sirens, you must immediately pull over to the right edge of the road and stop until they pass.

🚌

School Buses

If a school bus is displaying flashing red lights and a STOP arm, traffic in both directions must stop, unless separated by a physical median.

🚶

Pedestrians

Pedestrians in a marked or unmarked crosswalk always have the right of way. Even if they cross illegally (jaywalking), you must yield to avoid hitting them.
